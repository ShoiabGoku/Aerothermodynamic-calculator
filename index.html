All five models solve the same conservation-based normal-shock (frozen for ideal/real, chemical equilibrium for 3/4/5-species) so every difference is physics, not numerics — mirroring the fixed-Mach fidelity ladder.

Isentropic flow relations

Perfect gas, γ from the bar above. Enter any one quantity to get all the others; A/A* has separate subsonic and supersonic branches.

Normal shock relations

Perfect gas, γ from the bar. The upstream flow must be supersonic (M₁ ≥ 1).

Oblique shock — wedge / compression ramp

Real-gas oblique resolves the shock-normal Mach into the equilibrium normal-shock solver (tangential velocity preserved).

Conical shock — Taylor–Maccoll (perfect gas)

Sharp cone at zero incidence. The shock angle is found by shooting the Taylor–Maccoll ODE; surface flow is uniform along rays.

Prandtl–Meyer expansion fan

Isentropic expansion through angle Δθ (perfect gas, γ from bar): ν(M₂) = ν(M₁) + Δθ.

US Standard Atmosphere 1976

Valid to 86 km geometric. Returns T, p, ρ, speed of sound and viscosity.

Trajectory / parameter sweep — stagnation heating & shock

Sweeps the aerothermo solver to plot stagnation T₀, heat flux (Fay–Riddell & Sutton–Graves) and shock stand-off — e.g. along a re-entry trajectory.
Perfect-gas tools (isentropic, normal/oblique/conical shock, Prandtl–Meyer): Anderson, Modern Compressible Flow; NACA Report 1135. Real-gas aerothermo: thermally-perfect air + equilibrium air via statistical-thermodynamic dissociation constants (Vincenti & Kruger; Anderson, Hypersonic & High-Temperature Gas Dynamics). Heat flux: Fay–Riddell (1958) & Sutton–Graves (NASA TR R-376). Stand-off: Billig (1967). Atmosphere: US Standard 1976.
Open for any compressible-flow or aerothermodynamics study — for verification & education, not a substitute for converged CFD.
