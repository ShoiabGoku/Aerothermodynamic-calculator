All five models solve the same conservation-based normal-shock (frozen for ideal/real, chemical equilibrium for 3/4/5-species) so every difference is physics, not numerics — mirroring the fixed-Mach fidelity ladder.

Models: perfect gas (Anderson, Modern Compressible Flow); thermally-perfect air with vibrational excitation; equilibrium air via statistical-thermodynamic dissociation constants (Vincenti & Kruger; Anderson, Hypersonic & High-Temperature Gas Dynamics, Ch. 11). Heat flux: Fay–Riddell (1958) & Sutton–Graves (NASA TR R-376). Stand-off: Billig (1967). Surface pressure: modified Newtonian.
